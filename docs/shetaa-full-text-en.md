# Kurdistan Herbalists Association - Bylaws and Program

Kurdistan Herbalists Association (KHA)

Arabic name: الجمعية العشابية الكوردستانية

Kurdish name: کۆمەڵەى ڕووەکناسانى کوردستان

Approved by the first congress of the association, held on 15 March 2014 in Sulaymaniyah.

## Main slogan

Reconciliation with nature is comfort for the soul and body.

Nature is the greatest pharmacy.

Returning to nature is comfort for the soul and body.

The fields of complementary health are another gate toward public wellbeing.

## Introduction

The association is based on the idea that Kurdish society has a long cultural relationship with plants, nature, prevention, public health awareness and traditional knowledge. The association works to organize this field in a responsible, legal, scientific and educational way.

The website presents the association's official identity, bylaws, program, activities, research areas, official records and documents. Any health-related information should be treated as public awareness and historical/cultural information, not as a substitute for professional medical care.

## Part One: Name, head office, logo and slogan

### Article 1: Name and definition

Name: Kurdistan Herbalists Association.

The association is an independent professional and cultural association. It works to spread health awareness, prevention culture and responsible knowledge related to plants, nature and complementary health fields in South Kurdistan.

### Article 2: Head office

The main office of the association is in Erbil, the capital of the Kurdistan Region. Branches may be opened in governorates and representatives may be appointed in districts and subdistricts after approval by the Ministry of Interior and relevant authorities.

### Article 3: Logo

The logo contains two snakes around a plant stem, two leaves, the Kurdistan flag, and the association name in Kurdish, Arabic and English.

### Article 4: Slogan

Nature is the greatest pharmacy.

Returning to nature is comfort for the soul and body.

Complementary health fields are another gate toward wellbeing.

## Part Two: Objectives

1. Introduce the history of authentic Kurdish health knowledge, physicians and traditional healers to new generations and to the world.

2. Raise public awareness about health, prevention and responsible natural knowledge.

3. Encourage scientific study and documentation in cooperation with universities, scientific centers and relevant institutions.

4. Protect and increase wild and cultivated medicinal plants.

5. Develop members' abilities through writing, translation, research, exhibitions, conferences and seminars.

6. Establish an encyclopedia of Kurdistan medicinal plants and prepare a Kurdish herbal dictionary.

7. Invite specialists from inside and outside the Kurdistan Region to enrich the field.

8. Work against unqualified and unscientific practices that harm the reputation of the profession.

9. Coordinate with institutions, organizations and specialists for knowledge exchange.

10. Work toward legal recognition and professional regulation of complementary health professions.

11. Seek support from higher education, health, agriculture, industry and energy institutions for study, research, development and production related to medicinal plants.

12. Open awareness courses on food and its relationship with human health.

13. Work toward establishing farms and local production projects for medicinal plants.

## Part Three: Membership, conditions, rights and duties

Every citizen of South Kurdistan may join the association as a cultural member or a professional member.

Cultural members support the association as friends and helpers. Professional members work within the association's professional instructions and must satisfy the required age, character, education, training, pledge and legal conditions.

Members have the right to vote, stand for association roles, express opinions, make constructive criticism, attend meetings, and receive support in professional matters according to the bylaws.

Members must renew membership, respect the bylaws and decisions, attend meetings, participate in activities, work for the goals of the association, and provide subscriptions or support according to ability.

## Part Four: Loss of membership and penalties

Membership may be lost when a member does not comply with lawful decisions, violates professional conditions, harms the unity or reputation of the association, works without the required permission, resigns, or misses three consecutive meetings without valid reason.

Penalties may include warning, notice, suspension of membership rights, and dismissal. The member may appeal within fifteen days.

## Part Five: Challenges and legal methods

The association may address problems through proposals, follow-up, objection, peaceful public expression, or any constitutional and lawful method.

## Part Six: Income and finance

Income may come from monthly subscriptions, member contributions, identity and professional-permit fees, support from charitable and governmental organizations, activities, projects and publications. The association's finance is subject to official audit.

## Part Seven: Expenses

Expenses may include research, scientific trips, writing and translation, publications, travel for qualified persons, hospitality, rent, fuel, equipment, office administration, courses, lectures, exhibitions, seminars, conferences, congresses, awards and other necessary costs.

## Part Eight: Structure of the association

The association consists of the general body, administrative board, president, vice president, departments and branches.

The general body is the highest authority. It may amend the bylaws and program, unite with another association, dissolve the association and elect leadership. A congress is held every two years.

The administrative board consists of five main members and two reserve members. It manages membership, budget, annual programs, evaluation, decisions, committees and accountability.

The president represents the association and signs official correspondence. The vice president acts when the president is absent according to the bylaws.

Departments cover administration, accounting, media, relations, scientific research and courses.

Branches may be formed in governorates and local areas. They may accept and renew memberships, represent the association locally, submit reports, organize courses and seminars, keep records and defend members' rights.

## Part Nine: Records

The association keeps official records for members, accounting, decisions, assets and property. It also keeps records for incoming and outgoing correspondence, projects, activities, visits, meetings and related work.

## Part Ten: Dissolution

The association may be dissolved by a qualified decision of the general body or by court decision. In case of dissolution, its assets are transferred to a similar association according to the bylaws and law.
